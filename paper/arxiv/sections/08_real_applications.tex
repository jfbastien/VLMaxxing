\section{Streaming and Real Applications}

The most dramatic gains appear when the model keeps revisiting nearly the same
visual state. Deployment-scale evaluations on a larger Gemma 4 26B stack
report 0.8\,s median same-video follow-up latency at 10--18$\times$ speedup,
paired Streaming VideoMME results with 13$\times$ fewer ViT calls and
\mbox{$\sim$50$\times$} less dominant-pipeline compute, and selected
real-video regimes with 4.2--4.5$\times$ end-to-end speedups. A separate
large-$N$ exactness result shows byte-identical Qwen outputs across 1,937
sparse benchmark items when unchanged regions are reused instead of being
re-encoded. Live-camera measurements then show the same regime dependence in
the wild: ViT savings range from 5$\times$ to 300$\times$ with scene activity,
and the post-fix RTSP demo still reduces ViT calls by 14.3$\times$ over an
active 60\,s window.

The smaller-$N$ UI and live-demo studies matter because they expose the same
regime in more legible form. On current screen-recording case studies,
piecewise cache-shift preserves 75--88\% fresh-oracle judged answers while
reusing 87--99\% of the cache. The live RTSP demo v2 shows the same principle
on an active scene rather than an idle one. Over a 60\,s stovetop-cleaning
window, the pipeline decoded 701 frames, marked 34\% as novel, issued only 49
ViT calls, and therefore achieved 14.3$\times$ fewer ViT invocations than a
dense per-frame path. Across 11 queries, the output trajectory tracked the
physical sequence from pan pickup to cloth cleaning to spray-bottle use and
finally moving a pot back onto the stove; this is qualitative evidence and it
is labeled that way.

The current streaming evidence does not yet close a full systems-reviewer
checklist. The paper still lacks the screenshot-polling baseline, broader UI
diversity, and a dedicated event-detection benchmark. What it already does show
is deployment relevance: the same anti-recomputation principle continues to
matter when the input is an actual stream rather than a frozen evaluation
slice.
