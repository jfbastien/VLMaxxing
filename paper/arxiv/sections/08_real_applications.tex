\section{Streaming and Deployment Evidence}

The most dramatic gains appear when the model keeps revisiting nearly the same
visual state. Deployment-scale evaluations on a larger Gemma 4 26B stack
report 0.8\,s median same-video follow-up latency at 10--18$\times$ speedup,
paired Streaming VideoMME results with 13$\times$ fewer ViT calls and
\mbox{$\sim$50$\times$} less dominant-pipeline compute, and selected
real-video regimes with 4.2--4.5$\times$ end-to-end speedups. These are
companion measurements, not local headline rows. A separate imported companion
exactness result reports byte-identical Qwen outputs across what the companion
whitepaper reports as 1,937 sparse benchmark items under a separate
sparse-sampled QA protocol when unchanged regions are reused instead of being
re-encoded. Live-camera measurements then report the same regime dependence in
the wild: ViT savings
range from 5$\times$ to 300$\times$ with scene activity, and the post-fix RTSP
demo still reduces ViT calls by 14.3$\times$ over an active 60\,s window. This
is the operational regime where the thesis matters most.
That exactness row is not a live-decode streaming cell; it is a separate
sparse-QA protocol and stays labeled that way in the paper.

Smaller-$N$ UI studies stay strictly illustrative, not headline-bearing. The
sectional-scroll case studies remain explicitly weaker than the paired
streaming and real-video measurements above, and the contact-sheet imagery
below is qualitative rather than quantitative. The main-body evidence for this
section is the deployment table below.

The local Qwen bridge does not yet reproduce the companion Gemma deployment
story as a fidelity-preserving stack. The original stacked run reproduced the
speedup side but failed the accuracy budget. The dense-first-query admission
successor restores first-query aggregate correctness and leaves a
follow-up-only near-miss, but it also shows why the claim boundary must stay
strict. The long-bucket
continuations now measure follow-up image-token activity directly and find
none: all follow-up image tokens are cache-served under the current prompt-cache
path, and every tested long-Q0 keep rate misses the rescue band. That lane is
therefore dense-first-query admission plus K-cache reuse rather than proof of
active follow-up vision pruning. That is why
Table~\ref{tab:real-apps} stays labeled as deployment-scale companion evidence
rather than being folded into the local C-VISION or C-PERSIST headline rows.

The current streaming evidence does not yet close a full systems-reviewer
checklist. The paper still lacks the screenshot-polling baseline, broader UI
diversity, and a dedicated event-detection benchmark. What it already shows is
that the same anti-recomputation principle survives outside sparse benchmark
slices and into the regime where users actually keep asking the same system
about nearly the same scene.
