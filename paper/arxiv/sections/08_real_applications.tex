\section{Candidate C-STREAM and Deployment Evidence}

Native-rate streaming is the natural scale-out setting for anti-recomputation:
the model revisits nearly the same visual state while deciding when to keep,
shift, rebuild, or invalidate cached state. We keep this lane visible because it
is scientifically important, but it is not a fourth headline contribution yet.
The current release tree no longer reads a sibling deployment checkout or
free-form partner markdown. C-STREAM becomes claim-bearing only after a
validated local artifact bundle provides raw paired outputs, cache-correctness
smoke results, matched low-FPS or screenshot-polling baselines, and exact source
paths.

The local Qwen session/streaming bridge explains why this gate matters. The
best landed bridge restores aggregate first-query correctness and reaches
2.7869$\times$ paired speedup, but still misses the follow-up fidelity target.
Instrumentation also shows that follow-up vision pruning is inactive in that
family: follow-up image tokens are cache-served rather than actively pruned. The
right interpretation is dense-first-query admission plus K-cache reuse, not a
validated native-rate sparse-vision backend.
