\section{Conclusion}

The paper began with a small absurdity: much of the video did not change, and
the model paid anyway. The measured answer is not one single multiplier. Fresh
videos admit modest but real first-pass gains, and those gains obey a
share-limited ceiling that explains the regime and binds cleanly on the core
ceiling checks, including the matched Qwen cross-architecture probe. Same-video
follow-up queries admit much larger multipliers, already reaching sub-second latency on the local stack, and
selective re-prefill repairs the deeper cache-reuse basin: fixed \(K=1\) gives
no observed paired drift across 93 paired queries at 9.48--20.37$\times$
same-class follow-up speedup, while the adaptive post-\(Q2\) state policy
reaches 24.76$\times$ same-class speedup on the short slice. Separate
operational rows push the same idea further into streaming regimes with
4.2--4.5$\times$ real-video end-to-end speedups, double-digit ViT-call and
follow-up-latency reductions, and \mbox{$\sim$50$\times$} dominant measured
subpipeline savings.

The scientific value is in the boundaries as much as in the wins.
Fresh-compute placement over time cannot be reduced to novelty magnitude alone,
aggregate accuracy can hide answer-identity drift, and semantic substitution
must not be confused with measured sparse execution.

What remains is a measured sparse backend, broader benchmark coverage on the
second architecture, cleaner systems baselines, adaptive selective-reprefill
breadth, and a cache-invalidated follow-up test that directly explains why
reused prompt-cache state drifts. The n=60 composition null and the
long-bucket admission sweep already answered the easiest bad question: the
gains do not multiply naively. The useful question now is where each ceiling
moves in the architectures and deployment protocols people actually care about.

It also leaves a larger media agenda. The current paper is not an AI-native
codec and does not claim one. It shows why that direction is worth studying:
VLMs should not have to reverse-engineer every repeated frame back into state.
Future media streams for machines could expose change, motion, uncertainty,
objects, text, sensor time, and active tiles directly. Anti-recomputation is
one empirical step from video-as-frames toward video-as-world-state updates.
