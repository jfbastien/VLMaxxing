\section{Conclusion}

This paper argues for anti-recomputation, not for one particular trick. Fresh
videos admit modest but measured first-pass gains, and those gains obey a
share-limited ceiling. Same-video follow-up queries admit much larger
multipliers, already reaching sub-second latency on the local stack. Streaming
deployments push the same idea further into operational regimes with
double-digit to \mbox{$\sim$50$\times$} savings. The Qwen routing results
explain why the bars differ so much: fresh-compute placement over time matters
more than novelty magnitude alone, and semantic substitution must not be
confused with measured sparse execution.

What remains is composition, broader architecture coverage, and a cleaner
deployment bridge, not proof that the principle exists.
