Video vision-language models (VLMs) already receive evidence about what stayed
still, what moved, and what is new. Most stacks discard that structure, hand the
model dense RGB again, and ask it to rediscover state it could have reused. The
factory wall did not move, the stream has cheap evidence that it did not move,
and the stack still pays again for dense visual or prefix computation. We study
that waste as training-free anti-recomputation.

The largest local result appears after the video has already been ingested. On
frozen Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct-4bit, adaptive same-video follow-up reuse repairs
20-frame short, medium, and long VideoMME slices plus a 32-frame short slice
with no observed paired choice or correctness drift on 93 paired queries,
including 62 repaired follow-ups. Same-class follow-up speedup is
14.90--35.92$\times$ when cold follow-ups are compared with repaired
follow-ups; the cold-all-query versus repaired-follow-up ratio is
15.28--35.97$\times$. The mechanism is concrete: adaptive \(Q3\) inherits the
post-\(Q2\) repaired cache, so it appends mostly the new question instead of
repairing the newest-frame tail again. This is not first-query speedup: the
first query still pays full freight, and the tested sessions have two
follow-up questions per video. A separate many-turn stress on seven 20-frame
short videos then cycles the same questions through 50 turns: adaptive
post-\(Q2\) reuse and scheduled refresh have no observed paired drift on 343
follow-ups, while fixed \(K=1\) remains below the 3\% drift gate but shows
nonzero late drift.

The central question is where a runtime must buy fresh visual evidence over
time, and where cached state is enough to remain on its regime-specific
quality--compute frontier. That question only makes sense if the accounting is
honest. We evaluate frozen
VLMs on local MLX runs over VideoMME, MVBench, and TOMATO, plus a separate
26B-class streaming protocol, and we keep the regimes separate: first-pass
visual work on fresh videos, after-ingest follow-up questions about the same
video, and streaming workloads that revisit nearly the same visual state. They
are not interchangeable. \emph{Stage-share ceiling (C-CEILING)} formalizes the
bound: a component win becomes an end-to-end win only in proportion to the
wall-clock share it actually accelerates. This is why the n=60 composition
audit is a ceiling-matched null rather than a multiplier.

\emph{Persistent follow-up reuse (C-PERSIST)} is the name we give to this
after-ingest regime. It reuses the already-paid prompt/KV state for later
questions about the same video. Unrepaired reuse eventually enters a cache
basin, a failure region where reused state drives repeated answer drift or
pathological outputs. Selective re-prefill refreshes a small newest-frame
visual tail and restores the tested follow-up contract. Fixed \(K=1\) remains
the no-coordination baseline at 9.48--20.37$\times$ same-class speedup with no
observed paired drift on the same 93-query envelope.

Fresh-video pruning is smaller but measured. \emph{First-pass vision pruning
(C-VISION)} reduces work inside the VLM vision tower, the visual encoder that
turns frames into model tokens, during the first query on a fresh video. The
clean VideoMME holdout cell gives 1.113$\times$ first-pass wall-clock gain
with zero aggregate accuracy delta; advisory MVBench and TOMATO holdout cells
are larger but carry timing and accuracy caveats. Measured sparse vision
execution makes the first-pass story sharper: on Gemma 32f short, the runtime
skips timed vision-tower work at 1.316$\times$ first-query speedup with no
paired drift on 20 items and no parse failures. The full Gemma 8/16/32f sweep
has zero paired answer drift but matched dense/sparse parse failures in some
cells; Qwen is configuration-sensitive, with aggressive pruning failing
fidelity and a conservative 16f operating point restoring fidelity at
1.032$\times$ with only 13.6\% vision-time reduction. These are measured
sparse-execution envelopes, not a license to multiply every component win.
The routing audits explain why those numbers are not the whole story:
local diagnostics suggest fresh-compute placement is not captured by novelty
magnitude alone,
aggregate accuracy can hide answer-identity drift, and semantic substitution is
not itself a measured sparse backend.

Candidate C-STREAM is the native-rate version of the same question, but it is
not yet a headline claim. A checked 26B/M5 artifact bundle now makes the lane
more concrete and more bounded: default mixed sliding-window/full-attention
cache reuse fails a cross-turn correctness smoke, a topology-aware prefix
snapshot gives positive small-\(N\) follow-up rows, fixed-evidence streaming
baselines favor low-FPS dense over the current event-window proxy, and the
large exactness export separates zero accuracy delta on 1,937 rows from
byte-identical raw-paired text on 513 rows.

The broader implication is not that pixel differencing is the final interface.
The experiments are a requirements probe for future VLM-native media streams:
systems that expose change, motion, uncertainty, object state, sensor time, and
active tiles directly, so the model does not have to rediscover the world from
dense RGB every frame.
